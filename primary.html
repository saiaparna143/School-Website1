Courses In primary

We are offer to three schedules

Grade-1

Age Range: Usually 5 to 6 years old

Grade-1 Grade 1 marks the beginning of formal primary education, typically for children aged 5 to 6 years old. Students are introduced to core subjects such as mathematics, language arts, science, and social studies through a balanced curriculum that integrates hands-on learning, storytelling, and foundational academic skills development.

Grade-2

Age Range: Usually 6 to 7 years old

In Grade 2,The curriculum emphasizes literacy and numeracy skills development, critical thinking, and inquiry-based learning through interactive projects, group discussions, and collaborative activities.

Grade-3

Age Range: Usually 7 to 8 years old

Children typically enter UKG around the age of 4, completing their pre-primary education before transitioning to primary or elementary school.

Grade-4

Age Range: Usually 8 to 9 years old

Grade 4, typically for children aged 8 to 9 years old, emphasizes interdisciplinary learning and deeper understanding of core subjects. Students engage in project-based learning, critical analysis, and independent research to develop problem-solving skills and academic mastery across various disciplines.

Grade-5

Age Range: Usually 9 to 10 years old

Grade 5 serves as a bridge to middle school, typically for children aged 9 to 10 years old, preparing students for the challenges of upper primary education. The curriculum integrates advanced academic content with opportunities for leadership, collaboration, and personal growth, setting the stage for future academic success.

Grade-6

Age Range: Usually 10 to 13 years old

Middle school encompasses grades 6 to 8 and serves as a transitional period between primary and high school. Students aged 10 to 13 engage in a more comprehensive and specialized curriculum, with a focus on core subjects such as mathematics, science, language arts, social studies, and elective courses. Middle school prepares students for the academic rigors of high school while nurturing their social-emotional development and fostering a sense of independence and responsibility.

Grade-7

Age Range: Usually 14 to 15 years old
Grade 9 and Grade 10 (High School): High school, comprising grades 9 and 10, is a crucial stage in a student's academic journey, typically for ages 14 to 15. The curriculum becomes more specialized, offering a wide range of subjects including advanced mathematics, sciences, humanities, languages, and elective courses. High school prepares students for higher education or vocational pursuits by developing critical thinking, research, and communication skills, while also providing opportunities for extracurricular involvement, leadership development, and career exploration.
